Section B — Brand Identity

The Tribe.

Human. Transparent. Accessible. A trade association that looks like it belongs in any industry — because it does. Modern, colorful, clean. The most radical thing cannabis can do is stop looking like cannabis.

Direction B3 of 3 Voice Human (10) Weight Minimal (1) Reasoning Emotional (10) Openness Transparent (10)
B3.01
Archetype Mix

Through The Tribe Lens

The same archetype weights reinterpreted for a world where authority comes from clarity, wisdom comes from simplicity, and transformation happens when cannabis finally looks like every other modern industry.

55%
Ruler
The Accessibility Standard
The Tribe's Ruler sets standards through accessibility and clarity, not through weight or exclusion. Authority comes from making things simple enough that anyone can participate. The Chamber doesn't gatekeep through complexity. It opens doors by making the industry legible to operators, investors, and policymakers alike.
"The standard is: everyone can understand this."
30%
Sage
The Simplifier
The Sage here educates by making complex things simple. Regulation, compliance, market access, policy: none of it needs to be opaque. The Chamber's Sage takes the dense and makes it bright. Every position paper, every data release, every framework is designed so a first-time founder and a 20-year veteran get equal value from it.
"If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough."
15%
Magician
The Perception Shift
The Magician transforms perception by showing cannabis can look like any other modern industry. No special pleading, no counter-culture signaling, no "but we're different." The transformation is normalization. When an investor opens a Chamber document and it looks like something from Stripe or Notion, the argument is already won before a word is read.
"The most powerful statement is looking like you belong."

B3.08
Signature Device

The Color Bar

Three thin horizontal lines in green, coral, and dark. A closing mark that grounds every surface with institutional weight. Clean, warm, modern. It appears once per surface as the Chamber's signature.

The Cannabis Chamber of Commerce
The Color Bar

Three horizontal rules: Forest Green (#1B5E3B), Coral (#E85D4A), Midnight (#111827). Each 2px height, stacked with 3px gaps. Spans 40% of container width, centered. Border-radius 2px on each line. The three-color system anchors the institutional palette across every surface.

Application

Footer of digital pages, bottom of print documents, closing slide of presentations. Always centered. Always followed by the Chamber name in Inter 500 at 9px, tracked wide. This is the closing mark. It replaces traditional rules, borders, and seals with something that says: we are a modern institution.

B3.09
Creative Principles

Design Rules

Six rules that separate The Tribe from the other two directions. These are commitments, not preferences. Break them and the direction becomes something else entirely.

B3.09.01
Clarity is the Standard
Make complex things simple. If an operator can't understand it in 5 seconds, redesign. Regulation, compliance, market data, policy positions: none of it earns the right to be opaque. The Chamber's value is legibility. Every surface is a test of whether the industry can communicate like a modern one.
B3.09.02
Two Colors, Full Range
Green is institution. Coral is signal. Never decorative. Every color choice in The Tribe direction carries intention. The two-color system is not about restraint for its own sake. It is a functional language where green grounds and coral activates, making information instantly scannable and emotionally clear.
B3.09.03
Accessible by Default
Every surface, every message, every interaction designed for the widest possible audience. No gatekeeping through complexity. No jargon walls. No insider language that makes a first-time founder feel like they arrived late. The Tribe's Ruler sets the standard through inclusion, not exclusion.
B3.09.04
White Space is Confidence
Minimal weight, maximum impact. The Tribe doesn't fill space to prove it belongs. It leaves room because it knows it does. Every page, every document, every slide should feel like it could breathe further. Density is the opposite of authority in this direction.
B3.09.05
Human at Every Scale
From the favicon to the billboard, this should feel like it was made by people who care. Not a committee. Not a design system on autopilot. The rounded corners, the friendly type, the warm green: every detail is a decision to treat the audience as a person, not a target demographic.
B3.09.06
The Quiet Provocation
Edge (8) without volume (2). The most radical thing in cannabis is looking like you belong in any industry. No counter-culture signaling. No special pleading. The Tribe's provocation is normalization itself. When the Chamber's materials look like they came from Stripe or Google, the argument about legitimacy is already over.
